Interdisciplinary Chemistry Training

Based on my MSci Chemistry training at Imperial College London, I have developed a strong interdisciplinary foundation across organic, inorganic, physical, medicinal and materials chemistry. My coursework covered molecular structure and bonding, spectroscopy, thermodynamics and kinetics, electronic structure, reactivity at carbon centres, synthetic strategy and selectivity, as well as medicinal chemistry and macromolecular science.

Through extensive laboratory training, I gained hands-on experience in synthetic chemistry, analytical characterisation, reaction optimisation, experimental design, data analysis and scientific reporting. Advanced modules further strengthened my understanding of molecular systems, solid and liquid interfaces, and modern research methodologies, while research-focused training developed my ability to critically evaluate scientific literature, formulate research proposals and communicate complex chemical concepts effectively.

Collectively, these experiences have equipped me with strong problem-solving skills, quantitative analysis abilities, and a research-oriented mindset.

Cobaltocene-Mediated Activation of Sulfur Hexafluoride for Fluorine Transfer and Mechanistic Elucidation

Final Year Project Imperial College London · Oct 2025 – Jun 2026 · Supervised by Prof. Mark Crimmin
  • Developed a cobaltocene-mediated fluorine transfer strategy using SF₆ — a potent greenhouse gas — as a sustainable fluorine source for organic synthesis
  • Conducted air-sensitive organometallic reactions using glovebox and Schlenk-line techniques; performed substrate scope exploration across sulfonyl and acyl chloride acceptors
  • Investigated reaction mechanism through NMR spectroscopic monitoring and single-crystal X-ray diffraction; proposed a fluoride transfer pathway involving key reactive intermediates
  • Successfully scaled up to preparative scale and isolated fluorinated products via column chromatography
Glovebox & Schlenk Line ¹H / ¹³C / ¹⁹F NMR Column Chromatography X-ray Diffraction Sustainable & Green Chemistry Organometallic Synthesis

Development of Novel Peptide Substrates to Expand E3 Ligase Specificity & Synthesis of a Core Molecular Glue Skeleton for Targeted Degradation of ZBTB21

Research Intern Shanghai Institute of Organic Chemistry, CAS · Jul – Aug 2025 · Supervised by Dr. Zhixiang Chen
  • Designed and synthesised a library of peptide substrates via Solid-Phase Peptide Synthesis (SPPS) to modulate E3 ubiquitin ligase specificity for targeted protein degradation (TPD)
  • Characterised all peptides using LC-MS, HPLC and ¹H NMR; identified high-affinity ligand candidates for broader-spectrum degrader development
  • Contributed to a molecular glue project targeting ZBTB21 degradation through multi-step organic synthesis; purified target compound to >98% purity via preparatory HPLC
SPPS LC-MS Prep-HPLC Multi-step Organic Synthesis Chemical Biology Drug Discovery · TPD

Application and Characterisation of Deep Eutectic Solvents (DES) for Green Organic Synthesis
